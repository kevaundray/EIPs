## Abstract

Define a single window, `UNIFIED_RETENTION_WINDOW = compute_weak_subjectivity_period(state)`, evaluated against the latest finalized beacon state, and use it for every protocol-level retention obligation.

## Motivation

Ethereum currently defines four independent retention-style windows:

- `MIN_EPOCHS_FOR_BLOB_SIDECARS_REQUESTS` — 4,096 epochs (~18 days), from data-availability assumptions.
- `MIN_EPOCHS_FOR_BLOCK_REQUESTS` — 33,024 epochs (~5 months), a static upper bound on the WS period.
- Execution-layer history retention — varies across the [EIP-4444](./eip-4444.md) family.
- Weak-subjectivity (WS) checkpoint validity — the WS period itself, ~3,533 epochs (~15.7 days) at current mainnet.

Any window above the WS period is slack: older checkpoints are unsafe to sync from anyway. This EIP collapses all four onto `compute_weak_subjectivity_period(state)`.

## Specification

The key words "MUST", "MUST NOT", "SHOULD", and "MAY" in this document are to be interpreted as described in RFC 2119 and RFC 8174.

### Unified window

`UNIFIED_RETENTION_WINDOW` is `compute_weak_subjectivity_period` evaluated against the CL's **latest finalized beacon state**, with `SAFETY_DECAY = 10`.

### Consensus-layer changes

`MIN_EPOCHS_FOR_BLOCK_REQUESTS` and `MIN_EPOCHS_FOR_BLOB_SIDECARS_REQUESTS` are replaced by `UNIFIED_RETENTION_WINDOW`. A conforming CL node MUST serve blocks and blob sidecars (or, under PeerDAS, the columns it custodies) for any slot satisfying `current_epoch - slot_epoch <= UNIFIED_RETENTION_WINDOW`. Older data MAY be served best-effort.

### Weak-subjectivity checkpoint validity

A WS checkpoint is valid for sync iff `current_epoch - checkpoint.epoch <= UNIFIED_RETENTION_WINDOW`.

### Execution-layer changes

EL clients MUST retain block headers, bodies, and receipts within `UNIFIED_RETENTION_WINDOW` and MAY prune older objects, superseding per-fork history-expiry constants from [EIP-4444](./eip-4444.md) and related proposals.

## Rationale

### Pinning to the WS period

A fixed retention window below the WS period leaves checkpoints analytically valid but operationally unusable; one above it (as `MIN_EPOCHS_FOR_BLOCK_REQUESTS = 33,024` is today) is pure slack, since older checkpoints are unsafe to sync from anyway. Pinning to the formula eliminates both gaps and tracks the WS period across regimes where it moves (e.g. validator-set drops), with no constant to revisit at fork boundaries.

### Today's WS period

Above the churn-limit floor (`N > 4 · 65536 = 262,144`), the formula reduces to ~3,533 epochs (~15.7 days), independent of `N`, stake, or balance distribution; below it scales linearly with `N`. Mainnet has been well above for years, so the realistic upper bound is ~3,533 epochs. For blobs that's a drop from 4,096 — a ~14% reduction. Under PeerDAS the window applies to whatever blob data a CL node custodies.

### Corollaries

Divergences deeper than the window are handled by existing WS sync from a fresh checkpoint, not genesis-to-tip resync. During non-finality, `UNIFIED_RETENTION_WINDOW` is fixed at its last-finalized value; events exceeding it trigger WS recovery from the first post-recovery finalized epoch.

## Backwards Compatibility

This is a consensus-breaking change requiring a hard fork on both layers.

- CL nodes previously relying on peers for ~5 months of historical blocks (`MIN_EPOCHS_FOR_BLOCK_REQUESTS = 33,024`) must use WS sync from a fresh checkpoint instead.
- Blob sidecar retention shrinks by ~2 days (~14%).

## Security Considerations

### Operator planning

The WS period is bounded above by 33,024 epochs; operators SHOULD size storage against this ceiling. Implementations SHOULD retain `max(WS_period)` over a hysteresis window to avoid boundary toggling.

### Checkpoint-provider liveness

A CL node offline longer than the WS period cannot P2P-sync and must fetch a fresh trusted checkpoint. Ecosystems SHOULD maintain multiple independent providers; operators SHOULD pin recent checkpoints locally, especially under adversarial network conditions where a shorter window leaves less grace before sync becomes impossible.

### Data beyond the window

Access to data older than `UNIFIED_RETENTION_WINDOW` is out of scope, served by the Portal Network and archive nodes. This EIP assumes continued operation of at least one.
